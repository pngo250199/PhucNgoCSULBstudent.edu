Welcome to my Desktop Companion Project session

This project was my biggest research project with my team.

Introduce the project

The Desktop Companion device is a health monitoring accessory that uses biometrics to offer a more comfortable and productive desk experience. By building a device that would compact specific add-ons into one, it will eliminate the need to buy other multiple extensions that would be too costly and take up desk space. Some features include facial recognition for seamless login and gesture controls for activating hotkey functions to optimize workflow efficiency. This project was selected because we aim to design a compact device that will monitor the user’s health while providing the necessary feedback to improve productivity as the user is working at their desk. We are excited to do this, because we want to develop an accessory that engages with the user while monitoring their health.

What made this project special

The Desktop Companion is a LCD accessory for a Windows computer. It’s designed to monitor the user’s health by observing the user’s temperature and facial expressions by providing real time AI feedback based on that. The companion will also promote productivity with its widgets, activity tracker, and hand gesture control. Productivity is promoted with macros to control their desktop, real time feedback in regards to their activity level on the computer, and being able to tab between desktops with hand swipes. The companion’s widgets serve as hotkeys, or macros, so the user can open and minimize applications, control their media with media keys, and more. The companion’s activity tracker software will monitor metrics, such as daily keystrokes and clicks. This software will not store sensitive data, such as credentials, passwords, and etc. Lastly, the companion’s hand gesture software is used to detect hand swipes, and tab between the left and right desktop based on that. All of these features are designed to make the user have a more comfortable experience at their desk.

Widgets

The first features we developed is created unique widgets that completely separate with Stream-desk from the market. Our measures of success for this demo were to be able to customize the hotkeys on the Companion, and to use the hotkeys from the Companion and have them be executed on the computer. For this to be possible, we had to set up a socket communication between both the computer and the Companion. During our live demo, we first demonstrated our media keys, showing that we were able to control our music on the computer from the Companion. We also demonstrated the many applications that could be opened on the computer, such as slack, spotify, teams, text editors, and more. Lastly, we demonstrated the user interface and how to customize, add, and remove hotkeys from the Companion.

Health Monitor feature

After we finished the widgets, we implemented the 2nd microcontroller for the health monitoring features Our first measure of success for this demo was to be able to relay the user’s temperature and provide feedback based on their temperature. Our second measure of success for this demo was to be able to measure the user’s activity level by measuring metrics such as daily keystrokes, daily clicks, total keystrokes, and total clicks and provide feedback based on that. During our demo, we provided videos of the Companion measuring different temperature ranges. The Companion was able to record both average human temperature and when the user can potentially have a fever. For the average human temperature, the Companion notified the user that their temperature was good. When a high temperature range was recorded, the Companion notified the user that they could potentially have a fever. For our activity tracker, we were able to show the Companion consistently providing real time updates as the user’s daily activity increased. Additionally, we were also to show and explain why sensitive data isn’t recorded with the software.

Hand Gesture feature

The last feature of this project was combination of motion captures. we demonstrated the Companion reacting to the user’s facial expressions and the Companion providing real time updates based on that. We also showed our lock feature, by having the user walk out of the camera’s frame and locking the computer after 10 seconds had elapsed. For our hand gesture control, we showed a simulation of our program capturing hand swipes and displaying whether it was a left or right hand swipe. We also showed the Companion switching between desktops based on the hand swipe. Lastly, we were able to show an unified system by successfully demonstrating every feature in one go.
